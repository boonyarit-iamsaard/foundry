# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The personal portfolio of Boonyarit Iamsa-ard, a full-stack developer based in
Bangkok, Thailand. It serves four overlapping audiences, often arriving cold:

- **Hiring managers / recruiters** evaluating Boonyarit for a full-time role.
  They skim for quick signal of skill, experience, and reliability, and may want
  the resume in hand.
- **Engineering peers and leads** who judge depth — code quality, architecture,
  and reasoning — through the projects and long-form articles.
- **Potential clients** assessing whether to hire him for contract/project work.
- **Community readers** who land on an article via search and may stay to follow
  the writing.

Across all four, the visitor is forming a first impression of a person they
don't yet know and deciding whether he is worth their time and trust.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that earns trust through substance. It exists to convert a cold
visitor into someone who reaches out, remembers the work, or keeps reading.
Success is a recruiter requesting a conversation, a peer respecting the
engineering, a client feeling confident to hire, and a reader subscribing to the
writing — without the design ever getting in the way of those.

The site leads with a distinctive personal story (an aircraft mechanic who
taught himself to ship production software during the pandemic) and backs it with
real evidence: shipped projects, in-depth technical articles, and a clearly
stated stack. Story is the hook; demonstrated craft is the proof.

## Brand Personality

Calm, confident, and warm. Quiet authority over hype — the work and the writing
carry the weight, not loud claims or spectacle. Approachable and human: easy to
read, easy to talk to, inviting a conversation rather than performing at the
visitor. Precision is implied by the engineering itself, not announced.

Voice: plain, direct, generous with detail when it helps the reader. First
person, unpretentious. The kind of person you'd trust to own a hard system and
explain it clearly.

## Anti-references

The portfolio should explicitly NOT look like:

- **Flashy "creative developer" sites** — Awwwards-style scroll-jacking, WebGL,
  cursor trails, and heavy entrance choreography. Spectacle that undercuts
  engineering credibility and gets in the reader's way.
- **AI-slop SaaS landing pages** — gradient-text heroes, an uppercase tracked
  eyebrow above every section, identical three-card feature grids, the
  hero-metric template. Generic scaffolding with no voice.
- **Hacker-terminal gimmick portfolios** — dark "matrix/terminal" costume where
  the aesthetic is a gimmick standing in for substance.
- **Generic template portfolios** — an off-the-shelf "modern minimal" theme that
  looks like ten thousand other developers, with no personal point of view.

Positive direction: content-first, credibility-led technical sites in the spirit
of Milan Jovanović (milanjovanovic.tech), Laravel News, and Anton Dev Tips —
writing and work front and centre, editorial restraint, trust built through
substance.

## Design Principles

1. **Substance over spectacle.** Every visual choice must serve the work or the
   reading. If an effect competes with the content for attention, cut it. Motion
   is a quiet aid, never a performance.
2. **Show the work, don't claim it.** Demonstrate competence through real
   projects, real writing, and a clear stack rather than adjectives. Let evidence
   make the argument.
3. **Story is the hook, craft is the proof.** Lead with the unconventional path,
   then immediately back it with concrete engineering. Neither alone is enough.
4. **Editorial restraint.** Confidence through hierarchy, whitespace, and
   typography — not through density, decoration, or noise. When in doubt, remove.
5. **Respect the reader's time and trust.** Fast, legible, accessible, honest.
   The visitor is a stranger deciding whether to invest attention; never make
   that harder than it needs to be.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text meets ≥4.5:1 contrast (≥3:1 for large text),
including placeholder and muted text. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus
states, semantic HTML and landmarks, and meaningful alt text. Honour
`prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade or instant alternative for every
animation. Don't rely on colour alone to convey meaning.
