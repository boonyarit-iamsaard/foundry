# Site purpose: an online representation that demonstrates, doesn't solicit

## Context

While revising the skills/identity surfaces, we re-examined the site's purpose.
Boonyarit's capability has broadened (a confidential enterprise-insurance Spring
Boot/Java engagement alongside his primary TypeScript stack) and he describes a
"craftsman-founder" direction running _alongside_ an active goal of stepping up
into a more senior role. This raised the question of whether the site should
narrow to a single audience or conversion goal.

## Decision

The site stays a broad **online representation** — engineering judgment on
display, with the contact door open **both ways** (a role _or_ serious work).
We deliberately rejected narrowing it into a recruiter-first hiring funnel.

The governing boundary: the site **demonstrates and invites, it does not
solicit**. No "freelance/founder available" hard-sell, and equally no desperate
"hire me." The craftsman-founder direction is expressed as _character and
trajectory_ in the work, never as an offer or a CTA — neither hidden nor
advertised.

## Why

Narrowing to "step up the role first, founder hidden" was a narrowing Boonyarit
didn't want: it would erase the craftsman identity that makes him a stronger
senior hire _and_ quietly compounds toward the founder future. Keeping both
doors open lets the two ambitions compound instead of compete. Holding the
"demonstrate, don't solicit" line keeps this consistent with the brand's core
principle — _show the work, don't claim it_ — and avoids the open door reading
as a hustle, which would undercut the quiet authority the brand depends on.
