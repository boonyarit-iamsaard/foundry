## My Journey

My path into software is an unusual one. For nineteen years I worked as an aircraft mechanic — a trade where precision isn't a virtue you advertise but the baseline, because a careless mistake grounds a plane. That habit of careful, accountable work is what I carried with me when everything changed.

When the pandemic reshaped the world, I found coding and didn't look back. I taught myself full-stack development, and — still working as a mechanic — built an internal department system in Next.js and TypeScript to prove I could ship something real. That project settled it: this is the work I want to do.

## What I'm Working on Now

These days I work across two ecosystems. I'm deepest in TypeScript — React and Next.js on the front, Node.js behind it — and for the past year I've been just as hands-on in Java and Spring Boot, building services inside a large insurer's microservices systems. Different stacks, same instincts.

What I care about is building business systems people can trust: explicit structure, clear boundaries, and data that stays correct under pressure. I'm drawn to fintech and enterprise work here in Thailand, where getting the system design right matters more than any single framework.

## What I Work In

I keep a small, well-worn toolkit and go deep rather than wide. On the data side I'm strongest in SQL — PostgreSQL by choice, Azure SQL Server in the enterprise work — and I reach for it whenever the data has to stay dependable. Around that sit the everyday tools of shipping carefully: version control, continuous integration, containers, and tests on the flows that matter.
